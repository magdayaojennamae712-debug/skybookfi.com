Charter Holidays vs. Booking Your Own Flights

When to choose a charter package

Charter holidays work best for 1–2 week trips in November through February. Finnish tour operators TUI Finland, Aurinkomatkat and Tjäreborg run regular winter programmes to Tenerife. An all-inclusive week in December can cost €700–€900 per person including flights, hotel and meals — often cheaper than organising everything separately at peak season.

When to book your own flights

For shorter trips (long weekends), spring and autumn travel, or more flexible accommodation choices, booking your own scheduled flight with Ryanair or Norwegian gives you more freedom. Ryanair's direct Helsinki–Tenerife route offers some of the lowest fares available year-round. You can combine any hotel you choose and stay for exactly the duration you want.

Tips to Get the Cheapest Helsinki–Tenerife Flights

  • May and October are the golden months — excellent warm weather (24–25°C), the lowest prices of the year, and far fewer tourists than peak summer or winter.
  • Book winter flights 3–4 months in advance — December, January and February flights sell out early, often by September.
  • Compare charter packages vs. flight-only — for a full week in peak winter, an all-inclusive package from TUI or Aurinkomatkat can save €100–€200 vs. booking separately.
  • Fly to Tenerife South (TFS), not North (TFN) — TFS is much closer to the main beach resorts (Playa de las Americas, Los Cristianos, Costa Adeje).
  • Fly mid-week — Tuesday and Wednesday departures are typically €15–€30 cheaper than weekends.

Best Areas to Stay in Tenerife

Tenerife's south coast is the main holiday hub for Finnish visitors:

  • Playa de las Americas — busiest resort area with hotels, beach clubs and nightlife. Great for a lively holiday.
  • Los Cristianos — slightly quieter than Las Americas, popular with families. Good beach and harbour with ferries to La Gomera island.
  • Costa Adeje — upscale resort north of Las Americas. More sophisticated, with excellent restaurants and luxury hotels.
  • El Medano — quieter beach town, world-famous for windsurfing and kitesurfing. Great for active travellers.
  • Puerto de la Cruz (North) — charming old-town character, botanical gardens and Loro Parque. More cultural, but further from the airport (1 hour drive).

Top Things to Do in Tenerife

  • Mount Teide — Spain's highest peak (3,715m) and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Take the cable car for views above the clouds.
  • Siam Park — voted Europe's best water park. A full day of water slides and wave pools. Perfect for families.
  • Loro Parque (Puerto de la Cruz) — one of Europe's best zoos, with orcas, dolphins, gorillas and much more.
  • Masca Gorge Hike — dramatic hike through a volcanic gorge to a secluded beach. One of Tenerife's most spectacular natural experiences.
  • Whale & Dolphin Watching — resident pods of pilot whales and bottlenose dolphins live year-round in the waters between Tenerife and La Gomera.
  • Playa de los Gigantes — dramatic black sand beach beneath 600m volcanic cliffs. One of Tenerife's most iconic landscapes.
  • La Laguna Old Town — UNESCO-listed colonial city in Tenerife's north. Beautiful architecture, tapas bars and local culture.

Visa & Entry for Finnish Citizens

No visa required. Tenerife is part of Spain and the European Union. Finnish citizens can travel freely using their Finnish passport or national ID card. The currency is the Euro (€). No customs formalities apply when travelling within the EU.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long is the flight from Helsinki to Tenerife?

Direct charter and scheduled flights from Helsinki to Tenerife take approximately 5 hours 30 minutes. With one connection via Stockholm, Oslo or another hub, the journey is 7–9 hours depending on the layover.

What is the cheapest month to fly from Helsinki to Tenerife?

May and October offer the best combination of low prices and excellent weather — warm (24–25°C), sunny, and far less crowded than peak months. December, January and February are the most expensive due to winter sun demand.

Is there a direct flight from Helsinki to Tenerife?

Yes. Ryanair and TUI Finland both operate non-stop flights from Helsinki Vantaa (HEL) to Tenerife South (TFS), taking approximately 5 hours 30 minutes. Finnair also operates seasonal direct services. Charter operators run additional direct flights throughout the winter season.

Which airport in Tenerife should I fly to?

For beach holidays in the south (Playa de las Americas, Los Cristianos, Costa Adeje), always choose Tenerife South (TFS). Tenerife North (TFN) serves Santa Cruz de Tenerife and Puerto de la Cruz and is much further from the main resort areas.

Do I need a visa to visit Tenerife from Finland?

No. Tenerife is part of Spain and the EU. Finnish citizens travel freely with just their passport or national ID card — no visa required and no immigration formalities within the Schengen area.

What is the weather like in Tenerife in winter?

Tenerife's south coast stays warm and sunny year-round. In December and January, expect 22–23°C during the day with sea temperatures around 20°C. This is precisely why it is such a beloved winter escape for Finns facing −10°C or colder at home.
