💡 Pro tip: Ryanair flies to Rome Ciampino (CIA) which is 15km southeast of central Rome. Finnair flies to Fiumicino (FCO), which is Rome's main international airport. Both are well served by buses and trains to the city centre.

Tips to Get the Cheapest Helsinki–Rome Flights

  • Book Ryanair early – prices start very low and rise as the flight fills up. Book 2–3 months ahead for best fares.
  • Fly mid-week – Tuesday and Wednesday departures are often €20–€40 cheaper.
  • January, February and November are the cheapest months, with fares regularly under €100 return.
  • Avoid August – Rome is extremely hot and extremely crowded, and flights are most expensive.
  • Consider Ciampino airport – budget flights often land here; the bus into central Rome costs about €6 and takes 40 minutes.

Why Visit Rome?

Rome is the Eternal City – home to the Colosseum, the Vatican Museums, the Trevi Fountain and some of the world's best pizza and gelato. A long weekend in Rome is one of the most rewarding European city breaks you can take. Spring (March–May) and autumn (September–November) offer the best weather without peak summer crowds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a direct flight from Helsinki to Rome?

Yes. Both Finnair and Ryanair operate direct flights from Helsinki to Rome. Finnair flies to Fiumicino (FCO) and Ryanair flies to Ciampino (CIA). The flight takes about 3 hours 30 minutes.

What is the cheapest month to fly Helsinki to Rome?

January, February and November are the cheapest months. You can often find return flights for under 100 euros during these periods.
