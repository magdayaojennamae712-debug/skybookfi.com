Cheapest Time to Fly Helsinki to Manila (2025–2026)

📅 May 3, 2026 ⏱ 6 min read ✍ NordicWings

Flying from Helsinki (HEL) to Manila (MNL) is one of the most popular long-haul routes for people living in Finland — whether you're visiting family, going on holiday, or relocating. But prices on this route can vary enormously: from under €450 return in the low season to over €900 at peak times.

In this guide we break down exactly when to fly, when to book, and how to consistently find the lowest fares on Helsinki–Manila.

✈ Which Airlines Fly Helsinki to Manila?

There are no direct flights from Helsinki to Manila — all flights have at least one stop. The most popular routing options are:

  • Finnair + Philippine Airlines via Hong Kong or Doha — often the most convenient and competitive
  • Emirates via Dubai (DXB) — reliable, good business class, popular with Filipino-Finns
  • Qatar Airways via Doha (DOH) — award-winning service, often competitive on price
  • Turkish Airlines via Istanbul (IST) — one of the longest layovers but sometimes the cheapest fares
  • Cathay Pacific via Hong Kong (HKG) — good option, especially on flexible dates
  • Singapore Airlines via Singapore (SIN) — premium option, occasionally competitive

📅 How Far in Advance Should You Book?

Booking timing matters as much as travel timing on long-haul routes like HEL–MNL.

  • Best window: Book 2–5 months in advance for the lowest fares
  • Last-minute (under 2 weeks): Prices are usually 30–60% higher — avoid if possible
  • Too early (over 8 months): Airlines haven't released their best promotional fares yet
  • Sweet spot: 10–14 weeks before departure often hits a price dip
⚠️ Christmas warning

December flights are the most expensive of the year on this route — sometimes double the low-season price. If you must fly in December, book at least 6 months ahead and be very flexible with exact dates.

📆 Cheapest Days of the Week to Fly

Day of departure makes a real difference on long-haul tickets:

  • Cheapest days to depart: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday
  • Most expensive days: Friday, Sunday (business and leisure travel peaks)
  • Tip: Depart on a Wednesday and return on a Tuesday — this combination often saves €50–€100 compared to weekend departures

🛤 Best Stopovers on HEL–MNL

Since there's no direct flight, you'll always have a layover. Some stopovers are worth turning into a mini-trip:

  • Dubai (Emirates) — great for a 1–2 day Dubai layover if you have time
  • Doha (Qatar Airways) — Qatar offers free hotel stays for long layovers
  • Singapore (Singapore Airlines) — one of Asia's best stopover cities
  • Hong Kong (Cathay) — vibrant city, easy airport connections
  • Istanbul (Turkish) — fascinating city, Turkish Airlines offers free tours for long layovers

💡 7 Tips to Save Money on Helsinki–Manila Flights

  1. Be flexible with dates — even shifting by 2–3 days can save €100+
  2. Set price alerts on Google Flights or Kiwi.com — prices drop suddenly and briefly
  3. Compare nearby airports — sometimes flying via Tallinn or Stockholm adds connections but saves money
  4. Book during airline sales — Finnair, Emirates and Qatar all run seasonal promotions
  5. Avoid peak Finnish holidays — Midsummer (June), July, Christmas and New Year are expensive
  6. Check Kiwi.com "nomad" routes — they find creative multi-stop combinations that can be much cheaper
  7. Travel in February or November — these are consistently the cheapest months on this route

🌦 Weather in Manila — When Is the Best Time to Visit?

The cheapest flying months don't always match the best weather in the Philippines. Here's a quick guide:

  • November–April: Dry season — best weather, less rain, ideal for beaches and sightseeing ☀️
  • May–October: Wet season / typhoon season — some months are fine, but June–September can bring heavy rain and storms 🌧
  • Best overlap: November and February–March — cheap flights AND good weather
🏆 Best value combination

Fly in February or early March — you get low-season prices AND dry season weather in the Philippines. This is the sweet spot for Finnish travellers.
