Comparing the Best Routes to Bali

Via Dubai (Emirates)

Emirates flies Helsinki to Dubai daily with multiple onward connections to Bali. Total journey around 15–16 hours. Emirates economy includes 30 kg checked baggage — more generous than most competitors. Dubai airport (DXB) is well equipped for long layovers.

Via Singapore (Singapore Airlines)

The fastest option at approximately 14 hours total. Layovers in Singapore can be extended for a free stopover — explore Gardens by the Bay, the Jewel Changi waterfall, or the Marina Bay Sands rooftop. One of the world's most rewarding stopovers.

Via Istanbul (Turkish Airlines)

Turkish Airlines often has the lowest fares with 4–8 hour layovers in Istanbul. For very long layovers (over 7 hours), Turkish Airlines offers free hotel accommodation — making Istanbul an unexpected bonus city in your trip.

Via Doha (Qatar Airways)

Qatar Airways connects Helsinki to Doha daily with excellent onward connections to Bali. Doha's Hamad International Airport is one of the world's top-rated. Economy includes 23 kg baggage. Total journey around 15 hours.

Tips to Get the Cheapest Helsinki–Bali Flights

  • March, April and May are the sweet spot — Bali's dry season begins, prices are 30–40% lower than peak summer, and the island is far less crowded.
  • Avoid July and August when Finnish school holidays coincide with Bali's busiest season. Prices can be 50% higher.
  • Book 3–4 months in advance for summer travel and 6–8 weeks ahead for shoulder season trips.
  • Compare all hubs — Dubai, Singapore, Doha and Istanbul all vary in price week to week.
  • Consider a stopover in Singapore or Dubai — breaks up the journey and adds a bonus city for no extra flight cost.
  • Book return tickets — round-trip fares are almost always cheaper than two one-way tickets on long-haul routes.
  • Be flexible by 2–3 days — a small date shift can save €80–€120 on the same route.

Best Areas to Stay in Bali

  • Seminyak & Kerobokan — upscale beach clubs, boutique hotels, great restaurants. Best for couples and foodies.
  • Canggu — trendy surf town with cafes, yoga studios and a young international crowd. Best for solo travellers.
  • Ubud — cultural heart of Bali. Rice terraces, temples, cooking classes and the Monkey Forest. Best for culture and wellness.
  • Nusa Dua — resort area with calm beaches, 5-star hotels and easy airport access. Great for families.
  • Uluwatu — dramatic clifftop surf breaks and spectacular sunsets. Best for surfers.
  • Sanur — quiet, relaxed east-facing beach town. Ideal for families and a slower pace.

Top Things to Do in Bali

  • Tanah Lot Temple — iconic ocean temple on a rock. Must-see at sunset.
  • Tegallalang Rice Terraces — UNESCO-listed terraced rice paddies near Ubud, perfect for walks and photography.
  • Mount Batur Sunrise Trek — predawn hike up an active volcano with breathtaking sunrise views as your reward.
  • Uluwatu Temple & Kecak Fire Dance — clifftop temple with traditional Balinese fire dance performances at sunset.
  • Nusa Penida Day Trip — Bali's most photogenic island, home to the famous Kelingking Beach cliff and manta ray snorkelling.
  • Ubud Cooking Class — learn to make authentic Balinese dishes like satay lilit and nasi goreng with local chefs.
  • Surf Lessons in Kuta or Canggu — Bali offers some of the world's most accessible beginner surf breaks.

Visa & Entry for Finnish Citizens

Finnish passport holders can enter Bali on a Visa on Arrival (VoA) obtained at Ngurah Rai International Airport. The fee is approximately IDR 500,000 (around €30) for a 30-day stay, extendable once. Your passport must be valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned entry date. No advance visa required.

⚠️ Important: Indonesia has extremely strict drug laws with severe penalties. Travel insurance covering medical evacuation is strongly recommended — private hospital treatment in Bali can be expensive.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is there a direct flight from Helsinki to Bali?

No. All routes require at least one stopover, typically in Dubai, Singapore, Doha, Istanbul or Hong Kong. The shortest one-stop routes take about 14 hours total including the layover.

What is the cheapest month to fly from Helsinki to Bali?

March, April and May offer the best prices combined with good weather. Bali's dry season starts in May but June–August sees high prices. March–May is the sweet spot of low fares and improving weather.

How long is the flight from Helsinki to Bali?

The total journey from Helsinki (HEL) to Bali Denpasar (DPS) takes approximately 14–18 hours including the stopover. Singapore is the fastest routing (~14h); Istanbul is the longest (~17–18h).

Which airline is cheapest for Helsinki to Bali?

Turkish Airlines via Istanbul is usually the cheapest option. Emirates via Dubai is often a close second with more generous baggage allowance. Always compare current prices as fare availability changes frequently.

Do I need a visa to visit Bali from Finland?

Finnish citizens receive a Visa on Arrival at Bali's Ngurah Rai airport, valid for 30 days and renewable once. The cost is around €30. You need a passport valid for 6+ months and a return ticket.

What is the best time of year to visit Bali?

The dry season (May–September) has the best weather, but July and August are crowded and expensive. May, early June and September offer a great balance. October and November have occasional rain but far fewer tourists and lower prices.
