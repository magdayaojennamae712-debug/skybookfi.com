How to Find the Cheapest HEL–SIN Tickets

1. Book 6–10 Weeks in Advance

For economy tickets to Singapore from Helsinki, the sweet spot is booking 6 to 10 weeks before departure. Last-minute deals are rare on this route — airlines know it's popular and prices rise as the departure date approaches.

2. Be Flexible with Travel Dates

Flying mid-week (Tuesday or Wednesday) is typically €40–€80 cheaper than flying on weekends. Even shifting your departure by one or two days can make a significant difference on a long-haul route like HEL–SIN.

3. Use Fare Alerts

Set a price alert on Kiwi.com or Google Flights for the Helsinki–Singapore route. When the price drops to your target, you'll get an email instantly. This is one of the easiest ways to catch flash sales.

4. Consider a Two-Stop Route

Booking Helsinki → Istanbul → Kuala Lumpur → Singapore (or similar) can sometimes be €50–€100 cheaper, though it adds travel time. Check Kiwi's "Nomad" option which finds creative multi-stop routes.

5. Check Oulu and Tampere Departures

If you live in northern or central Finland, flying from Oulu (OUL) or Tampere (TMP) to Helsinki first and then connecting to Singapore may actually work out cheaper than booking the HEL–SIN segment alone — especially with Finnair combination fares.

🔥 Pro tip: Singapore's Changi Airport is one of the best in the world with free city tours during long layovers. If you're connecting through Singapore on the way to Bali, Phuket or Philippines, consider adding a day stopover — it's worth it!

What to Do in Singapore

Singapore is a world-class destination on its own — but it's also a perfect hub for onward flights to Bali, Bangkok, Manila, or the rest of Southeast Asia. Highlights include:

  • Marina Bay Sands — the iconic skyline and infinity pool
  • Gardens by the Bay — the famous Supertrees, free entry to outdoor areas
  • Hawker centres — Singapore's incredible street food culture at Lau Pa Sat or Maxwell Food Centre
  • Sentosa Island — beaches, Universal Studios and resorts
  • Little India & Chinatown — vibrant culture, temples and shopping

Singapore is expensive compared to the rest of Southeast Asia, but there are many free or low-cost attractions. Budget around €80–€150 per day depending on your hotel and lifestyle.

Book Your Singapore Hotel

Singapore has hotels for every budget — from Airbnb-style hostels in Chinatown to luxury resorts on Sentosa. Book early as central Singapore properties fill up fast, especially during Formula 1 season (September) and Chinese New Year.

Getting Around Singapore

Singapore has one of the best public transport systems in the world. The MRT (metro) and buses cover the entire city cheaply and efficiently. Get an EZ-Link card from any MRT station on arrival.

For day trips or travel to Malaysia (Johor Bahru), buses and trains connect easily from Singapore. If you're heading to Batam or Bintan islands in Indonesia, fast ferries depart from Harbourfront terminal.

Do You Need a Visa for Singapore?

Finnish passport holders do not need a visa to visit Singapore for tourism stays up to 30 days — entry is free and granted on arrival. Just make sure your passport is valid for at least 6 months beyond your planned stay.

Travel Insurance for Helsinki–Singapore

With flight times over 11 hours and connections through major hubs, travel insurance is strongly recommended. Look for a policy that covers flight cancellations, lost luggage and medical emergencies.
